Maple: What it is, what it does, and how it does it

What is it?

Maple is a MacOS runtime injection utility.
That's some serious lingo, let's break it down

MacOS

The modern OS that we all know and love, made by Apple

Learn More on Apple's website 

Runtime

The thing that makes things run, what starts your computer, runs your apps, drives your classes, and initiates your functions

Injection

The process of swapping out one objects functionality for another. This allows you to customize how your Mac runs

Sound good?

Install Maple

What's happening behind the scenes?

Step One

Developers disassemble and understand what your Mac does for certain actions. How functions, objects, and notifications work.

Learn Apple Development 

Step Two

They build new functionality to make your Mac do what you want it to. Nothing more. Nothing less. With line by line control, you'll never be left wanting features.

Step Three

They distribute a Leaf(Maple's file to tell it how to change your Mac), and you install it! Maple will do the replacement for you.

Make sense?

Get started with Maple

Time for a deep dive

Of course, you want to understand why this works! Maple uses a script to listen to new apps and daemons when they launch on your Mac, these are called processes. When a process launches which Maple has a Leaf for, it injects a Dynamic Library("dylib") into the loaded process. This dylib acts as the "swapper" and switches out the desired effects for the old ones.

Unfortunately, due to the nature of compiled code, it's difficult to know exactly what the new functionality is. I suggest ensuring you understand the Security Effects of enabling and installing Maple. See previous noted section for tips and suggestions on keeping yourself and your Mac safe.
